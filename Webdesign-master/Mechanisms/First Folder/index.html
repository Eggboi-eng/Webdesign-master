Dihalogenation

The two halogens end up adding opposite one another from the double bond substitution. There is no rearrangement due to the creation of a halogen bridge.

  • Markovnikov
  • Anti of the two Halogens
  • No Rearrangment

Hydrohalogenation

A halogen and -OH group end up adding opposite one another from the double bond substitution. The oxygen attaches to the most substituted carbon because if we think of the intermediate faze between the bridge breaking and the water attacking the molecule, the most electropositive region is at the more substituted carbon because the most substituted carbon is the most stable form of carbon cation, thus the less substituted carbon will have more of the electrons in the bond for a longer period of time. There is no rearrangement due to the creation of a halogen bridge.

  • Markovnikov (-OH group adds to the more substituted carbon atom)
  • Anti of the Halogen and the -OH group
  • No Rearrangment
